About
> What is LMUHacks?

LMUHacks is a hackathon at Loyola Marymount University (LMU) for LMU students, by LMU students! We are run by LMU's chapter of the Association for Computing Machinery (ACM), LMU's chapter of the Society of Women Engineers (SWE), and LMU's chapter of the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE). Additionally, LMUHacks is supported by LMU's Computer Science department and LMU's Career and Professional Development department.

> What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is an event where a group of people (or "hackers") form small teams and collaborate to build a minimum-viable product within a limited period of time. Usually, teams are encouraged to build a project with technology or resources that they are not familiar with, so that they can learn as much as possible. At the end of the event, teams show off their projects and can compete for prizes in a variety of categories.

> Why have a hackathon at LMU?

Firstly, hackathons have many benefits! They can provide those with little experience in a subject area with a beginner-friendly, pressure-free introduction to the topic. It also provides hackers with working projects that can be displayed on resumes, shown off to employers, and even continued into a larger-scale operation. And, of course, hackathons are a great way to meet new people, get some swag, and have a good time!

The purpose of a hackathon at LMU is not only to get all the benefits listed above, but additionally to promote interdisciplinary collaboration. LMUHacks is open to all majors, and specifically targeted at computer science majors, engineering majors, and animation majors. It also will teach students new skills that they can use in their future academic classes and professional careers.

Lastly, lots of other large schools have hackathons. Since LMU is growing and developing each and every year, we think it is a great idea for LMU to have its own hackathon that it can be proud of, and that great and creative ideas can be developed from.

FAQ
> I'm not a computer science major, engineering major, or animation major. Can I still attend LMUHacks?

Of course! All you need to attend LMUHacks is an interest in tech and an eagerness to learn.

> What should I bring?

Bring your laptop and charger (if you have one, if not, that's okay too), a reusable water bottle, comfortable clothes, and some energy!

> Is food provided?

Lunch and dinner will be provided for all, and at the opening ceremony we will have coffee and limited bagels. So, we recommend eating breakfast before you come.

> What are the workshops going to be?

The workshops are intended to give hackers with an interest in a subject they don't know much about a head start! Therefore, the two workshops that are currently listed as 'TBD' will be some sort of introduction to a platform. For example, an intro to making web-apps, an intro to making a game, etc.

> Am I able to attend if I can only stay for part of the time?

Participants who cannot attend the entirety of the event are not allowed to participate, since it is unfair to students who are able to attend the whole time. For questions on this policy, please email us.

> What swag am I going to get?

A t-shirt and some stickers!
